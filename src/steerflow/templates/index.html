What does this diagram show?

Imagine a "goal-space" made of multiple dimensions, where each dimension is an attribute of text like reading level or length. Users might ask LLMs to steer in goal-space, asking for things like "make this writing simpler" or "make it longer."

This flow diagram shows how LLM outputs shift in goal-space when try to steer them along specific goal dimensions. The x-axis represents the attribute we asked the model to change, while the y-axis shows how much an unintended attribute changed as a side effect. Arrows trace the average movement in this 2D space. The colors encode velocity, transitioning from cooler to warmer colors as speed increases. Ideally, vectors point mostly along the x-axis, indicating clean, disentangled control along goal dimensions.

What are these metrics?

This plot shows the three steerabiltiy metrics proposed in our steerability measurement framework:

  • Steering error (yellow): how far outputs deviate from the target,
  • Miscalibration (magenta): errors in the direction of intended movement, and
  • Orthogonality (blue): errors outside the direction of intended movement.
Lower values are better across all three (minimum: 0). The box and violin shapes show distributional spread across our steerability probe (see panel), and the numbers on top indicate the median and interquartile range (IQR; 75th - 25th percentile).
